## Folklore & Society: The Lopmoot Reckoning

Among the marsh-villages bordering the Whistlefen, the Lopsided Bunny-Faced Whiskerbeast is known as the **Lopmoot**, a name derived from its habit of bouncing lopsidedly along footpaths at dusk. Village record-keepers regard its asymmetry not as deformity but as *doctrine* — the mismatched eyes are read as proof that the creature perceives two separate truths simultaneously, one "round" (the seen world) and one "square" (the hidden world). Children born cross-eyed or with uneven features are historically welcomed into the **Order of the Off-Kilter**, a loose guild of diviners who claim kinship with the Lopmoot's crooked gaze.

Practically, the Lopmoot's stray whiskers are harvested — never plucked, only gathered after natural shedding — and bundled into "true-line brushes" used by local cartographers, since the whiskers are said to bend only toward unmapped ground. The pink inner-ear tissue, when dried, is ground into a pigment used to mark boundary stones, giving rise to the regional saying, "painted by the Lopmoot's ear," meaning a border no one dares dispute.

Symbolically, the creature's lack of visible limbs or body has made it an emblem of *acceptance without ambition* — a household charm called the **Bobbing Head** is carved in its likeness and hung above doorways to remind residents that contentment (that ever-present wavy smile) requires no reaching, walking, or striving. Its bouncing, rolling gait is imitated in the **Lop Dance**, performed at harvest to encourage crops to "settle unevenly but happily," much like the beast's own tilted ears — a folk metaphor for prosperity that need not be balanced to be whole.
