# *Equicaput numerocorpus*

## Anatomy

The Curly-Tailed Number Horse presents a striking case of morphological asymmetry between its cephalic and truncal regions. The head is a slender, wedge-shaped structure of unmistakably equine character, mounted asymmetrically at the front-left of a comparatively massive hexagonal body. A single keratinous horn-ear surmounts the skull—a fusion structure likely serving both auditory and display functions, though its solitary nature (rather than the paired arrangement typical of most vertebrates) remains taxonomically unusual. The lone black oval eye and triple-curved mouth striations suggest a face adapted more for signaling conspecifics than for stereoscopic vision or complex mastication.

The torso itself is the animal's defining feature: a scalloped, bump-fringed hexagon supporting eight short, uniformly spaced legs along its ventral margin. This leg count and arrangement produce a low-slung, skittering gait more reminiscent of myriapod locomotion than of any true equine.

## Diet & Life Cycle

*E. numerocorpus* is a generalist detritivore, using its fringed mouth to graze low vegetation and organic debris. The eight legs allow rapid, energy-efficient scuttling between forage patches. Little is known of juvenile development, though the fixed body markings (see below) appear stable from early instar stages onward, suggesting they are not seasonal or age-related pigment changes but fixed genetic expressions.

## Distinctive Adaptation: The Numeral Marking

Most remarkable is the thick-outlined numeral "3" borne centrally on the body—a natural pigmentation pattern with no known homolog in other species. Field observation suggests this marking functions in individual recognition among conspecifics, analogous to natural "ear-tag" polymorphisms seen in some ungulates, though here expressed as an apparently innate numerical glyph. Combined with the trailing, cursive-looped tail, the creature gives the distinct—if almost certainly coincidental—impression of having been catalogued by an unseen hand before birth.
